LIBTELCO will on January 18, 2013 launch Liberia first post war Fiber Optic Broadband cable

MONROVIA – January 16, 2013: The Liberia Telecommunications Corporation LIBTELCO will on January 18, 2013 launch Liberia first post war Fiber Optic Broadband cable.

ACE submarine cable was officially launched on December 18, 2012 in Banjul, Gambia and in the last thirty (30) days live traffic test has been ongoing in preparation for today’s commercial launch.

Following the launch of fiber optic the quality of internet and telecommunications will improve greatly in Liberia thus opening new corridors for economic growth and development in the country.

Fiber Optic will enhance post war Liberia development in diverse ways. To mention a few, it will afford superior and unhindered access to news and information; create new business and career opportunities for Liberians as well as greater access to global markets for Liberian goods and services. In addition, Liberians will have improved access to banking systems and services. The list goes on.

According to Ben Wolo, LIBTELCO’s Managing Director, the full implementation of broadband services to Liberia will rest upon the completion of the metro-Monrovia fiber optic backbone. At completion, this ring will loop the entire Monrovia and its environs. This fiber network will also off- shoot to Brewerville, Fendell, RIA and Firestone.

In keeping with LIBTELCO’s mandate to provide telecommunication services to all parts of Liberia and the Government of Liberia policy of providing Universal Access to all Liberians, the high speed broadband connectivity will be extended to other parts of the country gradually.

As a way of ensuring immediate and easy access to the high speed internet gateway, LIBTELCO will deploy wireless hotspot service through its base stations all across its coverage area.

For too long Liberians have encountered difficulties in using the internet due to poor quality in certain places, therefore, the government was committed to improving such condition with the launch of the Fiber Optic internet service.

ACE submarine cable, when completed, will span more than 17,000 kilometers along the West coast of Africa connecting 25 countries including Liberia.

Liberia’s position was secured as a member of the ACE Consortium, when LIBTELCO Managing Director Ben Wolo, signed the Construction and Maintenance Agreement (C&MA) on behalf of the Cable Consortium of Liberia (CCL) on June 5, 2010, in Paris France.

CCL is a Public Private Venture formed under a Special Purpose Vehicle to manage the ACE cable landing in Liberia.

This project, $25 million dollars in investment, was initiated by LIBTELCO and facilitated by the Government of Liberia. The current shareholders are LIBTELCO 20%; Cellcom and Lonestar 10% each, while the Government of Liberia (GoL) holds the remaining 60% that will eventually be divested.
