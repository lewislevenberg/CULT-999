Ghana reviews ICT policy

GOVERNMENT
Tweet
Image By Ghana Government
May 12, 2011, 11:26 a.m.
Ghana's deputy Communications Minister Ernest Armah

Ghana is reviewing its national ICT for accelerated development (ICTAAD) policy to meet changing priorities. Among the issues under review are broadband, cyber security, climate change and environmental and geo-information policies.

This is according to Ernest Attuquaye Armah, Ghana’ s Deputy Minister of Communications, who said the policy was also being streamlined and updated in light of new technological advances.

The deputy Minister was speaking on: “ Ongoing Policy Reforms in the ICT Sector-Opportunities for the Nation’ s Private Sector,” at a meeting of the Western and Central Regional Branch of the Association of Ghana Industries (AGI) in Takoradi in the Western Region.
   
He said to address the security issues associated with using ICT systems and the cyberspace, the Ministry was developing a Cyber Security Bill, which would take into consideration industry and business concerns.
       
He added that a Data Protection and Electronic Communications Regulations Bills were already before Parliament for consideration and passage into law.
       
He said the Ministry of Communications, in collaboration with the ICT industry, had created an enabling regulatory environment through the enactment of the Electronic Communications Act and the Electronic Transactions Act.
