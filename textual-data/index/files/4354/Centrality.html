We sort the ASes according to their centrality on the whole set of paths and represent them with different markers given the category they belong to. Tables 1 and 2 show the percentages of paths (from and towards each ISP) which traverse Orange, based respectively on the whole set of v4 paths in our dataset and the set of preferred paths. They highlight the business correlation of ISPs on their transit habits in the region. Indeed, the fact that Orange owns shares (minority or majority) of the state owned telco tends to influence the transit ASes in West Africa. However, the effect is reduced or increased given the location of the country (high preference to transit via Orange for ISP1 vs. low preference for ISP2), and the shares owned by Orange (high preference to transit via Orange for ISP1 vs. low preference for ISP3).
