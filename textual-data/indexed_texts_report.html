    Abstract Africa has some of the poorest telecommunications markets in the world. Although Africa is now considered the fastest growing telecom sector in the world, it has a lot of work to do before it catches up to the rest of the world, and its ability to continue to grow hinges heavily on international or private investment. Like many parts of the developing world, mobile technologies have helped bring telephone service and Internet access to people who could not afford a landline or do not have access to one.
